Introduction

I am a PhD Candidate in Economic History at Utrecht University, and I am on the job market for the 2021-2022 season. My PhD research attempts to combine archival research, econometric methods, historical insights and economic theory to investigate the wealth and investment profile of the Dutch political elite in the late 19th and early 20th century, the drivers of democratization of the Netherlands, and focuses on the incentives of Dutch political elites. In addition, I work on political rent-seeking in the context of both active and passive suffrage expansion in late 19th-century the Netherlands, and I am investigating the effect of Dutch firms in Indonesia: I focus on their political connections, and the long-term development consequences of these colonial firms. I am also a part-time supervisor of MSc and BSc theses at Erasmus University (the Netherlands).

Research Interests

My research interests encompass economic history, political economy, political history, institutional economics. I also love methodology and statistics, programming and web design, and in particular, I love R. Over time, I created and maintain a number of R packages, which you can access here.

This website
